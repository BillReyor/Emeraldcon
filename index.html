EmeraldCon 2025

The Unofficial HOPE Afterparty

Event Dates

August 15–17, 2025
Coincides with HOPE_16 at St. John's University, Queens, NY

Venue

The Emerald
A classic dive bar located adjacent to the conference hotel. Known for its authentic Irish pub atmosphere, it's the unofficial gathering spot for HOPE attendees seeking a more intimate and relaxed setting.

Who Should Attend

  • Hackers & Tinkerers: Whether you're into hardware hacking, software exploits, or just love the culture, you'll find like-minded individuals here.
  • Security Professionals: From pentesters to red teamers, it's a great place to unwind and share war stories.
  • Tech Enthusiasts: If you're passionate about technology and its societal impacts, this is your crowd.
  • Creatives & Artists: The intersection of technology and art is a frequent topic of discussion.
  • Anyone Who Loves a Good Pint: If you're looking for a place to relax, network, and enjoy a drink, The Emerald is the spot.

Activities

  • Informal Talks: impromptu discussions on topics ranging from cybersecurity to open-source projects.
  • Live Demos: show off your latest project or learn about others' work in a casual setting.
  • Networking: meet new people, exchange ideas, and build connections that could lead to future collaborations.
  • Games & Challenges: participate in friendly competitions, from lockpicking contests to trivia nights.

Getting There

The Emerald is conveniently located near the conference hotel. It's within walking distance, making it easy to pop in between sessions or after a long day at the conference.

How to Participate

  • Show Up: no registration required. Just walk in and join the fun.
  • Spread the Word: share this information with fellow attendees and encourage them to join.
  • Contribute: if you have something to share—whether it's a project, topic for discussion, or just a good story—feel free to contribute.

Why Attend EmeraldCon?

While HOPE_16 offers a wealth of structured content, EmeraldCon provides a space for organic interactions and relaxed conversations. It's where the community comes together in a more personal setting, away from the formalities of the main conference.
